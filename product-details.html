What is Cold Pressed Groundnut Oil?

Cold pressed groundnut oil is a type of oil extracted from peanuts (groundnuts), using an Indian traditional cold press technique. The process involves slowly rotating and pressing groundnuts in temperature-controlled rooms, at temperatures no higher than 40Â°C, to extract the oil without causing any damage.

Cold pressing help to retain the natural flavor and aroma of the nuts while preserving their nutritional content.

Tips for Cooking with Groundnut Oil

Before using groundnut oil, be sure to store it in a cool and dry place away from sunlight. When cooking with it, be sure to use low to medium heat. High temperatures can break down the fatty acids present in the oil making it less effective in terms of health benefits. Similarly, you should avoid deep frying food with this oil as it has a low smoke point.

Health Facts about Groundnut Oil

Groundnut oil is one of the healthiest cooking oils available. It contains omega-6 and omega-3 fatty acids which are essential for our body. This oil is also rich in antioxidants that help in cell repair and protect against free radicals.
